The problem

Every drain in India
remembers the sachet.

The foil–plastic laminate was designed for nine months of shelf life. The pan masala inside turns over in fourteen days. The packaging stays for five hundred years.

PWM Rules 2016, Amendment 14 March 2024, bars microplastics from food-contact single-use articles. The category is already on a regulatory clock.

The scale
70,000,000,000
plastic sachets thrown to the earth every year — in one country.
Laid end-to-end, they would circle the equator eighty-four times.
Stacked, they would reach the stratosphere. None of them will decay in our lifetimes.
Introducing

MittiSeal.

A thirty-five gram-per-square-metre sachet paper. Six materials. Every one of them a plant. Every one of them Indian. Every one of them, on its own, food-safe, compostable, and made within a thousand kilometres of where the pan masala is packed.

The sachet is made from the waste of the industry that processes its contents. Areca husk — the same tree whose nut becomes pan masala — provides thirty per cent of the paper pulp. The other seventy is sugarcane bagasse.

What goes inside each sachet

Six plant-derived materials. No synthetic polymer. No bioplastic. No aluminium.

1. Chitosan
Food-contact inner coat. Oxygen barrier + natural antimicrobial. Germination-promoting on disposal.
3 gsm · Mahtani · Veraval, Gujarat
2. Cellulose nanofibrils
TEMPO-oxidized plant cellulose. The primary moisture and oxygen barrier, replacing foil.
2 gsm · ICAR-CIRCOT · Mumbai
3. Areca-bagasse paper
Structural base. 30% areca-husk waste + 70% sugarcane bagasse. Agricultural by-products, not trees.
35 gsm · Pakka Ltd · Ayodhya + Campco
4. Orange shellac
Natural lac-resin from the lac beetle. Moisture + grease barrier. Doubles as the heat-fusion seal.
4 gsm · D.M. Shellac · Kolkata
5. Tulsi & marigold seeds
Native Indian seeds embedded in the outer seed zone. Sacred tulsi, festive marigold.
Outer patch · Pepaa · Hyderabad
6. Starch paste
Food-grade binder that holds the seed zone in place. Dissolves in rain to release seeds.
Seed-zone carrier · Indian FSSAI positive-list

Made in India

Every gram is
grown here.

No imported PLA. No Chinese CNF. No European chitosan derivative. Every material sourced within a single country, and most of them within a single state of the pan masala factory.

India holds more than eighty-five per cent of world shellac production. Kolkata. Bagasse is the world's largest agri-waste stream. Ayodhya. Areca nut is an Indian monoculture. Karnataka. Kerala. Cellulose nanofibrils, the one advanced material in our stack, are pilot-produced at ICAR-CIRCOT Mumbai — India's national cotton research institute, on a NAIP-funded line.

The hidden innovation

The coating is the adhesive.

Every other compostable paper sachet uses a plastic heat-seal coating — the one loophole BIS's own problem statement explicitly flags. MittiSeal eliminates the coating altogether. The shellac outer layer, briefly flashed by infrared light, softens to its melt-flow range and fuses to itself under the jaws of a standard VFFS line.

Before
Coated roll stock, loaded onto standard Nichrome PV-215 VFFS line.
Flash
Brief infrared flash softens shellac past its melting range.
Close
VFFS jaw compresses softened shellac layers together.
Sealed
Autohesive bond. Peel strength target 3–5 N/15 mm.

Operationalises Chauhan et al., IIT Delhi, International Journal of Biological Macromolecules, 2025.
Provisional Indian patent — integrated VFFS process — filed Phase 1.

End of life

A littered sachet becomes a plant.

The seed-paste zone on the outer face embeds native tulsi and marigold seeds in a starch binder. Chitosan — one of the six materials — promotes seed germination. The architecture is degradation-synergistic. We have not engineered a sachet that fights its disposal; we have engineered one that uses it.

Biodegradation evidence: 17 peer-reviewed citations covering component-wise mineralisation across soil, freshwater, and marine environments. Marine-specific shellac and chitosan data is the honest gap, closed in Phase 2 at CIPET Chennai or CSIR-NIO Goa.

The first packaging metric that cannot be gamed.

t90days to 90 % mineralisation in ambient Indian soil
