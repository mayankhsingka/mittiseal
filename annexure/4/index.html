Annexure 04  ·  Seed

Seed integration & germination viability.

The plantable idea only works if the seed survives production and then finds a way to grow. Here is how it does both.

Four native species are embedded into the fibre at the wet-forming stage. They must survive a 110°C press and a 75°C wax cure, then sit dormant in a sealed pouch for up to nine months, then germinate on contact with moisture.

Species not used

Invasive and allergenic species were deliberately excluded. No genetically modified cultivars are used. Seeds are sourced from National Seeds Corporation or state-accredited vendors with germination certification.

4.2 Surviving the process

Thermal isolation.

Seeds are encapsulated in a starch-alginate pellet at 2–3 mm diameter before entering the slurry. The pellet slows heat transfer to the embryo during the 110°C press (<4 seconds contact).

Placement away from seal lanes.

Seeds are deposited only in the sachet’s central body, never within 8 mm of seal edges, keeping them out of the VFFS heated jaw.

Moisture barrier on the seed side.

The interior-facing side is lightly hydrophobic, preventing the product’s residual moisture from breaking dormancy during shelf life.

4.3 Germination results

87 %Tulsi germination, 14 days
72 %Marigold, 18 days
64 %Moringa, 21 days
9 moShelf viability retained

Samples were aged for nine months at 30°C / 65% RH (accelerated). Viability was measured against a fresh-seed control in sterile potting mix at 25°C. All four species cleared the 60% threshold that the team set as the minimum credible plantability claim.

We decided early that “plantable” had to mean plantable, not theatrical. Any species below 60% field germination was dropped from the formulation.
