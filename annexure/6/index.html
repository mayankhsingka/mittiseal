Annexure 06  ·  LCA

Life Cycle Assessment.

Cradle-to-grave impact, measured against the laminate MittiSeal is built to replace.

A simplified LCA was performed per ISO 14040 / 14044, using Ecoinvent 3.9 datasets and SimaPro, with field data from the pilot line. Functional unit: one thousand pan-masala sachets, 3 g fill, with equivalent 12-month shelf life.

6.1 System boundary

  • Agricultural residue collection & transport (within 150 km).
  • Pulping, forming, coating, printing at plant.
  • Converting, filling, distribution to regional depot.
  • End-of-life: 70% open environment, 25% MSW, 5% industrial compost.
  • Biogenic carbon counted at 1:1 with uptake credit.

6.3 Reading the numbers

MittiSeal wins decisively on climate, fossil energy and microplastic leakage — the three impacts most directly aligned with the problem this product is designed to solve. The one category where it performs worse, marine eutrophication, is driven by pulping effluent; closed-loop water treatment in the Year-3 plant is expected to close this gap to parity.

6.4 Avoided-burden credit

The base case also avoids 0.9 kg CO&sub2;e per 1,000 sachets of paddy-straw burning, counted as a displacement credit following CDM methodology AMS-III.E. This credit is not included in the headline 71% figure and is presented separately.
