Annexure 01  ·  Material

Composition & biopolymer formulation.

What the sachet is made of, in what ratio, and why each ingredient earns its place in the matrix.

MittiSeal is built from three streams the Indian agri economy already produces as waste: rice-straw cellulose, sugarcane bagasse, and arhar stalk fibre. These are bound by a plasticised starch matrix and finished with a plant-wax food-contact coating.

1.2 Why this blend

Rice straw as base.

India burns roughly 23 million tonnes of paddy residue each year. Using it as feedstock converts an air-quality liability into raw material at negative acquisition cost.

Bagasse for strength.

Sugarcane bagasse gives the sheet its tensile backbone — the same reason it already dominates the moulded-fibre tableware industry.

Starch as binder.

Corn-starch plasticised with glycerol gives a cold-sealable, heat-weldable film that degrades cleanly in soil within 90 days.

Wax as the last mile.

A thin carnauba layer replaces aluminium foil as the moisture and aroma barrier, while remaining compostable.

Every ingredient is either GRAS-listed, IS 9845 compliant for food contact, or both. No PFAS, no PE, no EVOH, no aluminium.
