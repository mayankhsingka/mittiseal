Annexure 02  ·  Process

Manufacturing process & line.

From agri-waste bales to sealed sachet — the eight-stage wet-forming line designed for retrofit in existing paper mills.

MittiSeal is intentionally manufacturable on equipment that already exists in India. The line is a modified moulded-pulp machine plus a low-temperature coating and sealing unit — no novel capex, no imported extruders.

2.1 Process flow

Agri-waste collection & baling.

Rice straw, bagasse and arhar stalks sourced within 150 km of the plant via farmer-producer organisations. Moisture brought to <14% before dispatch.

Shredding & cleaning.

Biomass is shredded to 20–40 mm, de-stoned and dust-separated. Silica is the main concern with rice straw and is removed here.

Alkaline pulping.

Soda pulping at 150°C with dilute NaOH, 90 minutes. Black liquor is recovered and used on-site for boiler feed.

Bleaching (TCF).

Totally chlorine-free bleaching with hydrogen peroxide. No AOX discharge.

Slurry blending.

Pulp is blended with plasticised starch dispersion, colourant and seeds under low shear to protect seed viability.

Wet-forming & pressing.

Slurry is vacuum-formed on mesh, hot-pressed at 110°C against patterned rollers to create sachet blanks with reinforced seal lanes.

Wax coating.

Carnauba-candelilla emulsion applied by gravure, 3–4 µm dry. Cures at 75°C — below the thermal threshold for seed damage.

Form-fill-seal.

Compatible with standard VFFS machines at up to 120 sachets/min. Seal temperature 115°C, dwell 0.4s.

2.3 Retrofit pathway

Existing moulded-pulp plants producing egg trays and food-service packaging already own 80% of the equipment MittiSeal needs. The incremental capex is the blending skid, the gravure coater, and a sachet-grade VFFS head — approximately ₹2.4 crore for a 6 t/day line.

The manufacturing story is boring on purpose. Boring is bankable. No exotic equipment means faster diligence and lower financing cost.
