Annexure 03  ·  Testing

Biodegradation & compostability.

The sachet has to disappear. Here is the evidence, by standard and by environment.

MittiSeal is designed to pass the most demanding end-of-life test of all — the one where a consumer flicks it into the dust at the edge of a road. The sachet must return to soil in the same place it is thrown, without an industrial composter.

3.1 Standards referenced

  • IS 17088 : 2021 — Specification for compostable plastics (BIS).
  • ISO 17556 : 2019 — Biodegradation in soil by oxygen demand.
  • ISO 14855-1 : 2012 — Aerobic biodegradation under controlled composting.
  • EN 13432 : 2000 — Industrial-compost reference framework.
  • ASTM D6400 / D6868 — Coated paper compostability.

3.3 Ecotoxicity

Residual compost tested per OECD 208 on Lepidium sativum and Lolium perenne. Seed emergence and shoot growth were ≥ 94% of control. Heavy metal content was below all EN 13432 thresholds.

3.4 Real-world field trial

A 500-unit trial was buried at three sites — a Bengaluru urban plot, a Jaipur semi-arid plot and a coastal Thiruvananthapuram plot. Across all three, visible structure was lost between days 70 and 110, and seeds embedded in the sachet germinated in 43% of recovered samples.

The sachet does not just disappear. It leaves something behind — a sapling, not a fragment.
