Annexure 07  ·  Economics

Unit economics & feasibility.

What the sachet costs to make, what it replaces, and how the business closes the gap.

A compostable sachet only matters if a pan-masala brand will actually buy it. This annexure sets out the cost structure at two scales and compares landed price against the incumbent laminate pouch.

At scale, MittiSeal closes to within 15% of an aluminium-laminate sachet. An EPR credit under India’s Plastic Waste Management Rules (~₹0.04/sachet avoided plastic) brings effective parity.

The economics are not heroic. That is the point. A sachet that costs 5× laminate will never ship at Indian pan-masala volumes. MittiSeal is built to be boring on the invoice.
